## 📈 Trends This Week

1. **Agentic AI dominates job descriptions.** Nearly 4 in 5 roles mention agent orchestration, autonomous workflows, or multi-agent systems. The shift from "add AI to product" to "build AI that acts" is now the default expectation.

2. **ServiceNow/Moveworks is the sleeper opportunity.** Three Principal PM roles in Mountain View focused on enterprise search, AI assistant, and agentic platform — all directly relevant to rec/search/ads PMs. Comp range ($198K–$348K) is competitive and the domain overlap is strong.

3. **Foundation model companies pay 2–3x but hire differently.** OpenAI median TC is $860K, Anthropic offers $460K base. But these roles increasingly want PMs who've shipped LLM-native products, not just traditional ML. The bar is research-adjacent.

4. **Recommendation/search/ads PM experience is a differentiator at Tier 2 companies.** Uber, Snap, TikTok, Stripe, and Databricks all have roles where ranking, personalization, and ML systems experience maps directly. These are the sweet spot for applied AI PMs.

5. **Remote is real but narrowing.** Netflix ($700K remote) is the outlier. Most prestige roles are hybrid in SF/Mountain View/LA. Startups like Sierra and Glean require 4+ days in-office.

## 🧭 Applied AI PM Analysis

*For: Applied AI PM (Rec/Search/Ads) · 3 years post top-MBA · Targeting Senior/Staff roles*

### Your Market Position This Week

You're in a strong position for a specific slice of this market. Of the 32 qualifying roles found this week, 11 (34%) are High Fit — meaning your rec/search/ads background, 3 years of PM experience, and top MBA directly match what they're looking for. The applied AI domain (recommendation, search, ads ranking) remains one of the most valued PM specialties because it combines real ML shipping experience with measurable business impact. However, the market is bifurcating: ~40% of roles now want LLM/agentic experience specifically, which is a gap you'll need to bridge.

### Where You're Strongest

- ✅ **Uber, Snap, TikTok, Stripe, Databricks** all have roles where your rec/search/ads ranking experience is a direct match. These companies value PMs who've optimized ML systems for business metrics — exactly what you've done.
- ✅ **Your domain is rare and valued.** Most PMs claiming "AI experience" have only used AI tools or managed chatbot features. You've actually shipped ML-powered ranking/recommendation systems. This puts you in the top ~15% of AI PM candidates.
- ✅ **Top MBA + 3 years applied AI = the sweet spot for Senior PM roles.** Companies like Scale AI, Databricks, and Uber are explicitly looking for 3–5 year PMs with ML product experience. You're not too junior and not too expensive.
- ✅ **ServiceNow/Moveworks Enterprise Search PM** is an unusually strong match — it's a Principal-level role focused on search quality, evaluation metrics, and AI-powered search at scale. Your search ranking experience maps almost 1:1.
- ✅ **Experimentation and metrics fluency** from ads/search gives you an edge. Most AI PM candidates can't articulate how to A/B test a non-deterministic system. You can.

### Where You'll Face Headwinds

- ⚠️ **Level gap for ~40% of roles.** 13 of 32 roles are Principal or Staff level, typically requiring 6–10+ years of PM experience. Your 3 years post-MBA puts you at Senior level. You can stretch to Staff at startups but Principal at FAANG+ is a reach.
- ⚠️ **LLM/GenAI shipping experience gap.** 72% of roles mention LLM product development. If your experience is primarily traditional ML (collaborative filtering, gradient-boosted ranking, embedding-based retrieval), you'll need to demonstrate LLM fluency. The market has shifted from "ML PM" to "LLM PM" in 18 months.
- ⚠️ **Foundation model companies (OpenAI, Anthropic, DeepMind) are a stretch.** These roles want PMs who've worked directly with researchers on model capabilities. Your applied/product-side experience is valuable but they often prefer candidates from other foundation model companies or with research-adjacent backgrounds.
- ⚠️ **People management.** ~30% of Staff/Principal roles require managing other PMs. If you haven't managed direct reports yet, this narrows your Staff-level options.
- ⚠️ **Agentic AI is the new table stakes.** 78% of roles mention it. If you haven't shipped an agentic product (autonomous workflows, multi-step AI actions), you'll need a credible story for how your ranking/recommendation experience translates.

### Competitiveness Advice

1. **[Gap]: 72% of roles want LLM shipping experience, but your background is traditional ML ranking.**
   → Build a side project shipping a RAG-based search or recommendation product within the next 4 weeks. Use your existing search/rec domain knowledge but layer LLM retrieval on top. Document the evaluation framework (precision, recall, latency tradeoffs). This bridges the "traditional ML → LLM" gap that hiring managers are screening for. Even a well-documented GitHub repo with a write-up counts.

2. **[Gap]: Agentic AI is in 78% of JDs but you likely haven't shipped an autonomous agent system.**
   → Reframe your existing work. Recommendation systems ARE a form of autonomous decision-making — the system decides what to show without human intervention. In interviews, articulate how your ranking system "acts" on behalf of the user (selects content, optimizes for engagement, adapts to feedback). Then show you understand the new agentic paradigm (multi-step planning, tool use, error recovery) even if you haven't shipped it yet.

3. **[Gap]: Staff/Principal roles want 6+ years but you have 3 years post-MBA.**
   → Target the 11 High Fit roles aggressively — these are Senior-level and match your experience. For the Snap Staff PM and ServiceNow Principal PM roles, lean on domain depth over years. Write a cover note that says: "I've shipped X ranking system that drove $Y impact" — domain expertise can override the years-of-experience filter at companies that value shipping over tenure.

4. **[Gap]: Foundation model companies (OpenAI, Anthropic) prefer research-adjacent PMs.**
   → Don't apply cold. Instead, invest in building relationships with PMs at these companies through AI PM communities (Lenny's Slack, Product School AI cohort, NeonNook). Your applied AI background is actually what these companies need more of — they have plenty of research-oriented PMs but fewer who can ship products to millions of users. Position yourself as "the PM who makes research real."

5. **[Gap]: ~30% of Staff roles require people management experience.**
   → If you don't manage direct reports, seek out opportunities to mentor junior PMs or lead a cross-functional pod at your current company. Even informal leadership (leading a PM guild, running a PM book club, mentoring an APM) counts. Document it. This is the easiest gap to close in 2–3 months.
